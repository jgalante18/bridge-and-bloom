About the Practice

A Space Built for Your Healing

Bridge & Bloom was founded on the belief that therapy should feel like a partnership - warm, collaborative, and rooted in respect for your unique story. Whether you're navigating a season of change, working through past experiences, or seeking tools to manage daily stress, this practice offers a safe, affirming space to do that work.

With a foundation in evidence-based approaches, sessions are tailored to your individual needs and goals. No two paths look the same, and that's exactly the point.

The Approach

How We Work Together

01

Free Consultation

We begin with a no-pressure 15-minute call to see if we're a good fit. You ask questions, I listen - no commitment required.

02

Intake & Goal Setting

Your first session is about understanding your story and what brings you here. Together we'll outline what meaningful progress looks like for you.

03

Ongoing Therapy

Weekly or biweekly sessions tailored to your pace. We revisit goals regularly to ensure the work stays aligned with your evolving needs.

04

Growth & Transition

When you're ready, we'll plan a thoughtful wrap-up - celebrating your progress and equipping you with tools to carry forward.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get there?

Our office is accessible to Center City Philadelphia via SEPTA Regional Rail, other major metropolitan areas like New York City via Amtrak, and the PA and NJ via I-76. There is parking on the premise.

Do you accept insurance?

We currently do not accept insurance, but we can provide a Superbill for reimbursement through your insurance.

What types of disorders do you treat?

My focus is on adolescents and emerging adults navigating identity formation and self-discovery. Throughout this stage of life, a lot of stress, anxiety, and depression may appear. CBT and other modalities are evidence based treatments for depression and anxiety.

What are the assessments measuring?

The assessments can identify processing disorders, learning disorders, ADHD, and memory difficulties. These findings can help to establish potential accommodations for students.
